Mahan Hall

COMPLETED 1907  ·  LIBRARY 1907–1973  ·  THEATER TODAY

NAMED FOR

Rear Admiral Alfred Thayer Mahan

SEPTEMBER 27, 1840 – DECEMBER 1, 1914

Naval Strategist  ·  Historian  ·  Author of The Influence of Sea Power upon History

THE NAMESAKE

Alfred Thayer Mahan graduated from the Naval Academy in 1859 and had an unremarkable active sea career — he was, by his own admission, not a natural seaman. But his transformation into the most quoted naval theorist in the world came at the Naval War College in Newport, Rhode Island, where he joined the faculty in 1885. Preparing lectures on naval history, he was struck by a revelation that organized the rest of his intellectual life: sea power — the combination of merchant marine, navy, strategic bases, and geographic position — was the decisive factor in the rise and fall of great nations.

He expanded this insight into his landmark 1890 work, The Influence of Sea Power upon History, 1660–1783, which argued that Britain's rise to global dominance was inseparable from its naval supremacy. The book was an immediate and extraordinary success.

Kaiser Wilhelm II of Germany ordered a copy placed in every naval officer's mess. The Imperial Japanese Navy studied it as a strategic bible. British naval officers cited it as validation of their own traditions. In the United States it gave intellectual foundation to the naval expansion programs championed by Theodore Roosevelt — who became one of Mahan's most fervent admirers — and directly influenced the construction of the Great White Fleet and the strategic importance placed on the Panama Canal. Mahan was elected president of the American Historical Association, one of the highest honors in American intellectual life, and was the first naval officer selected as an honorary member of Britain's Royal Navy's United Services Institution.

THE BUILDING

Mahan Hall was built in 1907 as the centerpiece of Ernest Flagg's Beaux-Arts rebuilding of the Academy's academic campus, designed alongside Maury Hall and Sampson Hall. From 1907 through 1973 it housed the Academy's main library, including the beautiful Hart Room reading hall that was the intellectual heart of midshipman life for six decades. When Nimitz Library was completed in 1973 to accommodate the growing collection, Mahan Hall was repurposed as a lecture hall and performance venue. Today it is home to the USNA Masqueraders — the Academy's theater organization — and hosts major lectures, ceremonies, and Brigade-wide events. The Hart Room still exists within.
